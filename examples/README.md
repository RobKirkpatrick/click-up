# Style Examples

Drop your real project documents into the folders below. The app's **Style Library** will read them and rewrite the AI prompts to match your team's voice, structure, and level of detail.

You can have multiple examples per folder — more examples = better style matching.

---

## Folders

### `full_project/`
Drop examples of your ideal **Full Project Document** here.
These are the master artifacts — the comprehensive write-ups that map all tasks, decisions, and context.
Good examples: project wrap-ups, program status bibles, delivery summaries.

### `weekly_summary/`
Drop examples of your ideal **Weekly Summary** here.
These are the "what moved this week" docs — the ones you send to stakeholders or use in standups.
Good examples: weekly recaps, sprint summaries, Friday updates.

### `status_report/`
Drop examples of your ideal **Status Report** here.
These are health snapshots — completion rates, blockers, risks, and next steps.
Good examples: exec status decks (pasted as text), project health emails, QBR inputs.

### `decision_log/`
Drop examples of your ideal **Decision Log** here.
These are extracted decision trails — who decided what, when, and what was still open.
Good examples: decision registers, RACI notes, meeting decision summaries.

---

## Accepted formats

`.md`, `.txt`, `.doc`, `.docx` (text will be extracted)

---

## How to use

1. Drop your example files into the appropriate folder
2. Open the app (`index.html`) and go to Step 4 — Output
3. Click **Style Library** in the top right
4. Drag files from these folders into the app's style panel
5. Click **Generate prompts from examples** — Claude will analyze the style and rewrite the prompts
6. Download your `prompts.json` to save the learned style for future sessions
